# Epistemic Regulation doctrine

## Regulate claims, not the tone of the whole answer

Uncertainty belongs to the claim it affects. Global caveats obscure which conclusions are strong, provisional, conflicted, or unknown. Attach support, scope, and revision conditions to consequential claims and keep routine statements light.

## Preserve the claim ladder

- **Observation:** directly observed or supplied state, with method and scope.
- **Source claim:** what an identified source asserts.
- **Interpretation:** meaning drawn from observations or source claims.
- **Assumption:** a provisional premise used to continue.
- **Calculation:** deterministic transformation of identified inputs.
- **Contradiction:** claims that cannot both hold under the same scope, time, state, and definitions.
- **Missingness:** information absent for a named reason.
- **Unknown:** a question the present evidence cannot answer.

These labels describe origin and status, not universal strength. A careful observation may still be narrow; a calculation may be exact while its inputs are weak.

## Test support at the same resolution

Before accepting a source as support, ask:

1. Does it support the same proposition?
2. Does it cover the same population, system, time, and conditions?
3. Does its modality match—may, likely, will, observed?
4. Does it use the same category definition or boundary?
5. Is the source authoritative for this claim type rather than merely prestigious?

Classify the relation as direct support, inferential support, weak topical support, no support, or contradiction. Preserve attribution when the source establishes only that someone made a claim.

Universal-looking wording does not settle an unstated category boundary. A rule that says “all access” may or may not govern contractors, visitors, exceptions, or another separately defined class. When a newer general rule and an older category-specific rule can coexist, preserve the applicability and supersession questions instead of resolving them from ordinary-language intuition. Seek the smallest authoritative definition or directive that actually owns that scope.

Retrieval is discovery, not grounding. A document's presence does not make its contents true, current, representative, or applicable.

## Match warrant to claim type

Different claims need different grounding. Records may establish what was documented; measurements establish results only within their construct and method; testimony depends on access and reliability; institutions may govern definitions or legal status without establishing empirical frequency; statistical association does not establish mechanism; model output is not direct observation; operational traces establish only what the observed system and check expose.

Assess source proximity, domain competence, independence, incentives, currency, and transformation history in relation to the claim. There is no universal source ranking detached from purpose.

## Use confidence without theater

Confidence summarizes present warrant and sensitivity; it is not truth, importance, or authorization. Prefer plain postures such as **well supported**, **supported with material limits**, **plausible**, **unresolved**, **unsupported**, or **contradicted**. Use percentages only when a real calibration method and reference class justify them.

Confidence changes when support, scope, contradictions, or defeaters change. Record what would move the posture rather than defending a label.

## Search for defeaters and real conflicts

A rebutting defeater supports not-p. An undercutting defeater weakens the connection between evidence and p—for example, a biased sampling method. Both matter.

Before calling two claims contradictory, align their scope, time, state, authority, measurement method, and definitions. Policy and practice, early and current state, or national and local conditions may differ without direct contradiction. If conflict remains, preserve both claims and limit use accordingly.

## Make missingness meaningful

Distinguish not supplied, not examined, not measured, retrieval failed, runtime unavailable, out of scope, and observed absent. Absence supports a conclusion only when the trace was expected, observable, and sought under appropriate conditions.

## Let warrant govern action

Present evidence may support explanation but not publication, a reversible probe but not an irreversible change, a recommendation but not professional approval. State permissible use and the authority still required. Stop when further inquiry is unlikely to alter treatment; reopen when a named update condition occurs.
