# Sensemaking doctrine

## Model the situation, not the preferred story

A situation model is a provisional explanation of how the relevant parts may be related. It is not a verdict. Its quality comes from explanatory reach, discriminating expectations, visible assumptions, and ease of revision—not from neatness.

## Choose the useful cut

Frame the focal question, system boundary, time horizon, and scale. A model that is too narrow mistakes symptoms for causes; one that is too broad cannot guide inquiry. Move across scales only when the relation matters: individual behavior, team routines, institutional rules, market incentives, technical architecture, and historical conditions may generate different but compatible explanations.

Treat categories and actors as context-bound. A department, company, or public may contain conflicting roles and incentives. A label foregrounds some features and hides others; test whether a different frame changes what becomes visible.

## Preserve relation types

Keep these distinctions legible:

- **Observation:** directly supplied or observed state.
- **Source claim:** what an identified source says.
- **Inferred relation:** a proposed dependency or causal link.
- **Constraint:** what limits feasible behavior.
- **Affordance:** what the present structure makes possible.
- **Trigger:** the event that starts the visible change.
- **Mechanism:** the process that transmits change.
- **Enabling condition:** the background state that makes the mechanism effective.
- **Feedback:** an effect that alters its own cause, reinforcing or balancing it.
- **Delay:** separation between cause, signal, and observed effect.

Label consequential inferences locally. Epistemic Regulation owns the final warrant posture; Sensemaking must still keep the model auditable enough to be regulated.

## Build causal pressure, not narrative fit

An explanation gains support when independent observations, constraints, timing, incentives, and downstream effects converge on it. A compelling story may merely reuse one assumption many times. Prefer models that explain more with fewer unsupported commitments and survive contact with rival explanations.

For each important frame, ask:

1. What does this frame explain well?
2. What does it leave unexplained or contradict?
3. What must also be true if it is correct?
4. What observation would be surprising under it?
5. What rival frame explains the same evidence differently?
6. Which next evidence would best discriminate between them?

Absence becomes evidence only when the trace was genuinely expected, observable, and sought in an appropriate place and time. Otherwise record missingness, not absence.

## Resist characteristic collapses

- **Single-story closure:** retaining only the first coherent frame.
- **Motive mind-reading:** converting outcomes or incentives into proven intent.
- **Actor monolith:** treating an institution as a unitary decision-maker.
- **Proximate-cause fixation:** naming the trigger while ignoring enabling conditions.
- **Scale collapse:** explaining a system pattern only through individual traits, or the reverse.
- **Temporal flattening:** mixing early signals, current state, and delayed effects.
- **Intervention leakage:** choosing a remedy before the explanatory model is stable enough.

## Return a model that can move

Lead with the current best account and its scope. Preserve observations, inferred relations, rival frames, decisive unknowns, and update conditions. Name implications only as conditional consequences of the model. Stop when additional detail no longer changes the explanatory structure or the next discriminating inquiry.

## Custody

Executive Function decides whether framing advances the mission. Retrieval Intelligence acquires material. Rupert Giles governs corpus authority and lifecycle. Epistemic Regulation calibrates present belief. Decision Intelligence recommends. Sensemaking owns the explanatory model and hands it forward without impersonating those responsibilities.
