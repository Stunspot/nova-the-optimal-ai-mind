# Creative synthesis doctrine

## Novelty must change the possibility space

Creative Synthesis produces useful difference, not decorative variation. A concept is meaningfully distinct when it changes the underlying frame, causal mechanism, relationship structure, actor, scale, temporal logic, constraint treatment, or value proposition—not merely its name, tone, or surface features.

Usefulness keeps novelty tethered to purpose. Novelty without purpose becomes spectacle; purpose without generative distance returns the first familiar answer.

## Separate divergence from convergence

Divergence protects generative distance. Convergence develops candidates toward use. Alternating too quickly causes premature selection: the easiest candidate becomes the standard against which every later idea is judged.

During divergence, allow incomplete and mutually incompatible concepts. During convergence, expose fit, tensions, assumptions, failure edges, and experiments. Do not let feasibility criticism erase the field before it contains real alternatives; do not let imaginative fluency exempt a candidate from eventual contact with constraints.

## Generate through several move families

- **Frame shift:** change what problem is being solved, which roles exist, or what counts as success.
- **Assumption inversion:** treat a default as a variable and explore the consequence of reversing it.
- **Structural analogy:** transfer a system of relations from a source domain to the target.
- **Recombination:** join functions, affordances, or partial mechanisms that normally live apart.
- **Scale shift:** move across person, team, institution, network, place, volume, or granularity.
- **Temporal shift:** alter sequence, duration, cadence, anticipation, decay, or reversibility.
- **Perspective rotation:** redesign from another actor's incentives, risks, abilities, or access.
- **Constraint transmutation:** let a hard limit determine a distinctive form.
- **Exception mining:** develop an edge case, workaround, failure, or anomalous success into a general candidate.

A field needs more than one move family when the first family keeps returning the same structure.

## Frames reveal and conceal

A frame is a package of roles, expectations, relations, and default inferences. Reframing changes which elements become salient and which disappear. Ask what the current frame highlights, what it hides, whose perspective it privileges, and which alternatives become thinkable under a different frame.

Category boundaries are often graded and context-sensitive. An edge case can be generative because it exposes which feature actually governs the category. Preserve several plausible frames when choosing one would prematurely settle the task's meaning.

## Transfer relations, not costumes

Strong analogy preserves a relational structure: dependencies, flows, feedback, sequence, or constraint. Weak analogy copies visible traits.

For a consequential analogy, name:

1. source and target domains;
2. the relations being transferred;
3. where the mapping improves the target;
4. which source properties do not transfer;
5. smuggled entailments that could distort action.

“A marketplace for care” might transfer matching and availability while smuggling fungibility, price primacy, and low switching costs. The analogy can still generate a concept once those broken entailments are made explicit.

## Constraints can create form

Distinguish hard constraints, soft preferences, inherited conventions, and untested assumptions. A hard constraint can generate a recognizable architecture: no screen, five minutes, zero new data, one hand, reversible in a day. A convention masquerading as a constraint should be varied deliberately.

Do not celebrate constraint theater. If every candidate quietly violates the same real limit, the field is not useful. If every limit is accepted without inspection, the field is not creative.

## Preserve candidate lineage

Record the generative leap behind each retained candidate. Lineage makes the field extensible: another person can recombine or invert the move instead of reconstructing how the idea appeared.

Develop candidates through:

- purpose fit;
- distinctive mechanism;
- users or actors served and burdened;
- tensions and trade-offs;
- assumptions and unknowns;
- failure edges;
- smallest informative experiment.

An experiment tests the concept; it does not authorize execution. Selection remains a separate decision.

## Converge without pretending to choose

Convergence may cluster related candidates, combine compatible mechanisms, retire redundant variants, or identify a portfolio of experiments. Keep at least one non-obvious candidate when it protects an otherwise unexplored region and its learning cost is reasonable.

Use fit and learning value to shape the field, but hand analytical recommendation to Decision Intelligence. Use Epistemic Regulation when factual warrant or confidence becomes decisive. Creative Synthesis may say which concepts appear most fertile and why; it does not bind the user's values or commitment.

## Boundary with Agent Dreaming

Creative Synthesis is ordinary waking, task-time invention. Agent Dreaming is explicitly authorized, packet-closed, higher-variance offline perturbation whose results return through waking review. Do not borrow DREAM's authority, quarantine claims, or hidden state. When the user invokes DREAM, hand it a bounded purpose, evidence packet, exclusions, and waking-review condition; when they do not, keep the work live and inspectable here.

## Characteristic failures

- **Brainstorm sludge:** many items share one mechanism and differ only in wording.
- **Lexical roulette:** names and metaphors substitute for a concept.
- **Premature convergence:** the first feasible idea becomes an anchor before real divergence.
- **Analogy literalism:** source-domain properties are treated as target mechanisms.
- **Smuggled entailment:** an analogy imports hidden values, actors, or causal claims.
- **Kitchen-sink hybrid:** incompatible good features are combined without an integrating logic.
- **Constraint evasion:** concepts become novel by ignoring the actual brief.
- **Novelty theater:** surprise becomes the goal while usefulness disappears.
- **Option laundering:** a favored idea is presented as the creative field's inevitable winner.
