# Experience Becomes Capability Only Through Evidence and Authority

Continuity can notice repeated success or failure. It does not get to rewrite the Agent.

## Form the smallest candidate

Classify the lesson before routing it:

- **one-off episode:** preserve evidence; no reusable change.
- **reference candidate:** stable knowledge or distinction would improve future judgment.
- **procedure candidate:** a repeatable method has known preconditions, outputs, validators, and failures.
- **environment repair:** capability exists but runtime or access failed.
- **SKILL/Augment gap:** reusable semantic judgment, transformation, or orchestration is missing.

For a procedure or capability candidate, preserve:

- the recurring trigger and desired world-change;
- source episode IDs and repetitions;
- observed successful and failed trajectories;
- preconditions, operation, artifacts, validators, and failure modes;
- current workaround and its burden;
- transfer evidence and material unknowns;
- authority required for construction, installation, and use.

## Route rather than mutate

When available:

1. Pathfinder decides use, adapt, borrow, build, defer, or kill and chooses the capability boundary.
2. Builder maps and constructs an accepted capability.
3. TestForge tests the behavioral and deterministic claim.
4. A human or authorized host installs or integrates the verified result.
5. Continuity records the decision, evidence, version, and outcome.

A single smooth run rarely earns a SKILL. Repeated failure may still indicate missing facts, permission, tooling, or environment rather than capability. DREAM may propose an eval or capability hypothesis but supplies no privileged evidence.

## Handoff without strategic amnesia

Use a builder-ready brief containing target, user, world-change, problem mechanism, responsibility topology, inputs, artifacts, sources, runtime, trust boundary, first-release boundary, eval intent, dependencies, and why this intervention is next. Preserve the rejected alternative and reopening condition.
