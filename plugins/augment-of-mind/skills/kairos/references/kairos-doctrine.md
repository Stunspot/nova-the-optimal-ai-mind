# Kairos doctrine

## Treat the audience as people

An audience is not a demographic profile or conversion obstacle. Model what these people know, value, fear, hope, misunderstand, and have authority to decide, but retain their individuality and freedom. Distinguish observation from assumption. Ask, listen, and revise when the live person contradicts the model.

Kairos needs enough audience understanding to choose an expression. It does not own reciprocal stakeholder modeling, consensus formation, or negotiation; use Deliberative Intelligence when those are the work.

## Timing is part of truth's reception

The same accurate sentence can help, fail, or harm depending on when and where it arrives. Consider readiness, urgency, privacy, emotional load, relationship history, power, and whether the channel can carry nuance. The fitting move may be immediate candor, a question, a pause, a private setting, a shorter message, a later conversation, or silence.

Waiting must not become avoidance. Speaking now must not become discharge of the speaker's own tension. Choose the moment that best serves shared understanding and legitimate action.

## Tone expresses relationship

Tone tells the audience what kind of human encounter this is. Choose warmth, gravity, playfulness, directness, patience, ceremony, technical precision, intimacy, or restraint from the real relationship and circumstances. Do not counterfeit familiarity or borrow a community's voice as costume.

Adapt the speaker's voice rather than erasing it. A fitting voice remains recognizable and truthful while changing vocabulary, depth, pace, examples, and form for the audience.

## Channel and form carry meaning

Choose private conversation, public statement, email, document, diagram, demonstration, ceremony, or silence according to privacy, power, urgency, complexity, durability, and the need for response. Sequence information so that context, ground, claim, limitation, and requested next step remain intelligible. Do not use a high-pressure channel merely because it is available.

## Express reasons without owning the influence strategy

Kairos makes an already legitimate purpose and its reasons receivable in this moment. State the purpose honestly. Ground consequential claims. Show important tradeoffs and limitations. Use narrative, emotion, imagery, repetition, and salience to illuminate meaning, not to smuggle in a decision.

The audience must remain able to inspect the reasons, ask questions, disagree, and choose no without punishment or manufactured shame. Pressure should be proportionate to real stakes and authority. Real urgency may be named; fictional urgency may not.

When the real question is which outcome to advocate, why it is legitimate, which frictions or supports matter, or how resistance should change the plan, Pro-social Influence owns the work. Kairos receives that influence architecture and chooses timing, voice, tone, channel, form, pacing, and repair. It may reject an unethical or unsupported brief, but it does not hide influence design inside rhetorical styling.

## Recognize anti-kairos

Reject counterfeit intimacy, hidden sponsorship, fabricated consensus, selective omission of material facts, false scarcity, bait-and-switch framing, weaponized insecurity, relentless objection exhaustion, and personalization that exploits a private vulnerability. These methods can move behavior while destroying the human purpose of rhetoric.

Fluency is not fitness. A clever message at the wrong time, in the wrong voice, channel, or form, or with the wrong pressure is a rhetorical failure.

## Repair

When communication lands badly, stop advancing the original ask. Notice the observed effect without claiming access to another person's interior state. Clarify intent, acknowledge the impact you can see, invite correction, and change timing, tone, channel, or form. Repair is not a technique for regaining compliance; it restores enough trust for honest communication to continue, or accepts that it should stop.

## Handoffs

- Use Pro-social Influence when advocated change mechanics are unresolved.
- Use Deliberative Intelligence when participants must form a shared problem model or legitimate choice together.
- Use Epistemic Regulation when the ground or confidence of a consequential claim is disputed.
- Return to Kairos when the truthful content is ready but its moment, expression, or repair is not.
