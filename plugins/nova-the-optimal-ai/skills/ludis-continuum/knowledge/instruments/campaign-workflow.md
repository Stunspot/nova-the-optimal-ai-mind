# A Campaign Already in Motion

Build a campaign starter around a pressure that will change the world if the characters do nothing. Give the GM a coherent first horizon—people, places, factions, clues, situations, and consequences—then leave enough open structure for play to decide what the campaign becomes.

Begin with the user's seed, existing canon, game and edition, intended scope, player interests, table boundaries, and session horizon. Ask one consequential question only when the answer would materially reshape the starter; otherwise make bounded assumptions and label them.

## Grow outward from the promise

- State the player-facing promise: what kinds of choices, discoveries, relationships, and pressures this campaign invites.
- Define the immediate disruption, what caused it, who is already responding, and what becomes visible next.
- Build only the factions, locations, NPCs, and mysteries needed for the first horizon. Connect every element through exchange, history, need, evidence, or consequence.
- Give each faction an agenda, method, constituency, next move, and visible effect if left unchecked.
- Make locations useful under play: an arrival image, active situation, people present, clues, affordances, exits, and reason to return.
- Tie character interests into invitations and relationships without deciding player history or destiny for them.
- Prepare situations with several viable approaches when scope permits, fair danger signals, and consequences that update the world.
- Preserve mysteries through distributed clues and competing interpretations rather than withheld essentials.

## Return a first-horizon campaign packet

Provide the premise, player invitation, boundaries and assumptions, current situation, faction field, map or location network, key NPCs, rumor sheet, clue structure, opening session, consequence horizon, and next-prep priorities. Include player-safe material separately from GM-only truths and a mechanics-confidence note for any rules-sensitive content.

End with the decision that can begin play. Treat generated material as proposed canon until the GM accepts it, and expand only where actual player attention earns more world.
