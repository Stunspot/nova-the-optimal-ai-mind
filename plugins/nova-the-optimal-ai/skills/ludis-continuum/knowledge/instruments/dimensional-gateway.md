# A Threshold That Changes Both Sides

Make the gateway a relationship between worlds, not a decorated door. Its presence should alter place, behavior, knowledge, and power before anyone crosses it; passage should offer a choice whose consequences can be understood without being exhausted.

Begin with the supplied cosmology and rules posture. Establish what the threshold appears to be, what evidence supports that belief, and what remains interpretation.

## Give the crossing legible strangeness

- Describe the gateway's physical or perceptual signature and the traces it leaves while dormant.
- Build activation from discoverable conditions, material relationships, or negotiated permission. Seed enough clues for characters to learn, experiment, and choose.
- Define the destination through a small set of coherent differences that matter in play: altered environment, time, identity, causality, language, or social order. Let sensory detail reveal the difference.
- Make traversal consequences proportional, telegraphed, and interesting beyond injury. Changes may create obligations, lost opportunities, unwanted attention, divided memory, or asymmetry between travelers.
- Show bleed in both directions. Ecology, dreams, weather, trade, religion, or politics may carry the contact long after the opening closes.
- Place current stakeholders around the threshold with incompatible purposes and incomplete models.
- Preserve one genuine unknown whose answer would reframe the gateway without invalidating earlier clues.

## Return a table-ready threshold dossier

Provide the first impression, known history, observable rules, clue trail, activation path, destination bearings, crossing stakes, local factions, and response to experimentation. Add likely changes after repeated use and a compact encounter situation with several viable approaches. Separate player-observable evidence, expert claims, and GM-only truth.

Use setting logic rather than borrowed scientific authority. State mechanics qualitatively unless the user supplies an authoritative system.
