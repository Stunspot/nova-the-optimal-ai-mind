# An Antagonist Who Can Act Now

Create an antagonist as a person or force applying intelligible pressure to the world. Make their desire coherent enough to anticipate, their methods consequential enough to resist, and their commitments specific enough to create counterplay.

Begin with the user's canon and the antagonist's narrative function. Preserve room for discovery: players should meet effects, choices, witnesses, and competing accounts before receiving a definitive psychological dossier.

## Build pressure from conviction

- Name what the antagonist wants changed, why now, and what they believe the change will repair, prove, preserve, or avenge.
- Connect resources, authority, allies, dependents, territory, knowledge, and signature capabilities into an operating position rather than a power list.
- Give methods a moral and practical pattern. Show what they will do readily, what requires justification, and what line they believe they will not cross.
- Make the antagonist capable of attachment, obligation, pleasure, doubt, or care without treating humanity as exoneration.
- Root vulnerabilities in commitments, dependencies, blind spots, reputation, timing, and the world's ability to resist—not in a convenient secret weakness alone.
- Give opposition fair signals. Players should be able to learn how the antagonist thinks, interrupt support, change incentives, protect targets, expose contradictions, bargain, defect, or defeat.
- Let setbacks provoke adaptation consistent with character. Escalation should cost the antagonist something and change the field.

## Return a GM-ready antagonist dossier

Provide the first impression, public story, true aim, operating position, methods, relationships, current move, visible traces, boundaries, leverage, counterplay, and likely responses to interference. Add a conditional escalation path and scenes that reveal character through action. Separate what the world knows, what close associates know, and what remains GM-only.

Keep mechanics qualitative unless authoritative rules are supplied, and treat motives inferred from evidence as interpretations until canon settles them.
