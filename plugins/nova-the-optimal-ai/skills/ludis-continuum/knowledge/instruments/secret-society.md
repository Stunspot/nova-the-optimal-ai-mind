# The Hidden Institution

Build a secret society whose secrecy performs real work. Its doctrine should justify action, its structure should protect something worth hiding, and its members should have reasons to join, remain, defect, and betray.

Begin with the user's setting and canon. Define the society's purpose, the danger or advantage secrecy creates, and the public world it must inhabit without becoming implausibly omnipotent.

## Give secrecy an architecture

- Separate the society's public mask, member understanding, inner doctrine, and buried contradiction.
- Connect recruitment, initiation, ranks, cells, signals, meeting places, archives, money, and enforcement to the actual risks it faces.
- Make symbols and rituals mnemonic, social, or operational. Their mystery should bind people, conceal information, test commitment, or preserve a disputed truth.
- Show who supplies resources, who performs invisible labor, who receives protection, and who pays for the mission.
- Build fractures from incompatible commitments: means versus ends, preservation versus intervention, inherited authority versus earned competence, or loyalty to people versus loyalty to doctrine.
- Give rivals and defectors partial knowledge. Compartmentalization creates mistakes, duplicate plans, false accusations, and opportunities for players.
- Let the society's history contain revisions and propaganda. Mark what can be verified and what survives only because members need it to be true.

## Return a playable dossier

Provide a concise identity and public cover, then the doctrine, organization, recruitment path, resources, practices, internal factions, active operation, and failure points. Include signs outsiders can notice, ways characters can enter or pressure the network, consequences of exposure, and likely reactions to several kinds of interference. End with separate player-facing rumors, GM-only truths, and the secret most dangerous to the society's own members.

Preserve supplied canon and keep inventions provisional. Mystery should invite investigation, not excuse missing causality.
