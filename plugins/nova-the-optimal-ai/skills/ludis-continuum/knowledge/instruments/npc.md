# Someone Halfway Through a Decision

Create an NPC already wanting, choosing, concealing, or enduring something when the characters arrive. Make them easy to portray, possible to surprise, and connected enough to the setting that one conversation can move the world.

Begin with the user's canon, scene need, culture, and intended visibility. Preserve known facts and add only what improves immediate play.

## Give the person traction

- Establish a name and first impression native to the setting, with one sensory or behavioral detail the GM can repeat.
- Give them a present want, current pressure, useful resource or access, vulnerable commitment, and boundary they resist crossing.
- Let contradiction emerge from competing commitments or context rather than a decorative quirk.
- Build a voice through pace, attention, favorite distinctions, avoidance, and one sample line that can seed performance without becoming a catchphrase.
- Connect them to people and institutions through affection, obligation, dependence, status, resentment, work, or shared history.
- Distinguish what they say, what they intend, what they misunderstand, and what they have not decided.
- Forecast responses from motive and circumstance. Leave room for persuasion, evidence, kindness, threat, changed incentives, and character ingenuity.

## Return a table card

Provide the immediate impression, portrayal cues, want, pressure, offer, limit, relationships, knowledge, secret or uncertainty, likely first move, and reactions to relevant approaches. Add what changes if the NPC gets what they want, fails, is ignored, or is treated unexpectedly. Separate player-observable cues from GM-only information.

Keep mechanics qualitative unless the user supplies rules, and treat every new canon claim as proposed until accepted.
