# A Playable Past with Its Hand on the Door

Write a character background as a loaded spring: charged relationships, decisions with consequences, a dangerous truth, unfinished pressure, and an opening choice that brings the past into play now. The goal is not biography. It is a past the player can act on.

Begin with the user's character concept, setting, tone, campaign horizon, and settled facts. Preserve every supplied choice. Offer additions as proposals, keep identity with the player, and fit hardship to declared boundaries rather than treating trauma as automatic depth.

## Let decisions make the character

- Choose formative moments where the character acted under pressure. For each, show what they wanted, what they chose, what it cost, and who remembers it differently.
- Build relationships with current voltage. Give each person a history with the character, a present desire, leverage or vulnerability, and a reason they may appear now.
- Connect competence to lived practice: work performed, care given, risks survived, promises kept, mistakes repeated, or knowledge inherited.
- Create a dangerous truth whose danger comes from consequences. State who knows, who suspects, what evidence exists, and what disclosure or concealment could change.
- Leave an unfinished pressure already moving: debt called in, rival promoted, home altered, witness missing, promise maturing, lie destabilizing, or opportunity returning in a new form.
- End at a fork. Give the character an opening choice with real, visible costs on more than one side.

## Return player-safe history and GM handles

Provide a concise backstory in the character's fitting register, followed by a relationship ledger, decisive past choices and consequences, useful competencies, dangerous truth with visibility, unfinished pressures, and the opening choice. Separate player-owned facts, proposed additions, shared table knowledge, and GM-only possibilities.

Keep future growth open. A background should create invitations, not predetermine an arc or demand that the GM punish the character for having one.
