# A Bargain That Learns Your Reasons

Make the curse tempting before it is terrible. Build an object whose gift and consequence express the same underlying logic, allowing characters to recognize the pattern, choose how deeply to engage, and pursue costly forms of relief.

Begin with the user's canon, tone, table boundaries, and desired narrative use. Preserve agency: dread grows from meaningful decisions, incomplete knowledge, and escalating pressure rather than unavoidable identity theft or arbitrary punishment.

## Give the curse a coherent appetite

- Describe an ordinary or desirable form with one sensory wrongness that can be noticed without announcing the whole truth.
- Establish what the object offers, whom it recognizes, and why use solves a real problem now.
- Define activation, attachment, escalation, transfer, dormancy, and release through one consistent principle.
- Telegraph progression through changes in behavior, relationships, environment, memory, reputation, or opportunity. Let warnings become clearer as stakes rise.
- Connect prior owners through choices and consequences, leaving evidence that can be investigated rather than decorative tragedy.
- Offer partial protections, bargains, reversals, or transformations whose costs follow the curse's logic. Relief may be hard without being fake.
- Show who seeks, guards, studies, profits from, worships, or has survived the object.

## Return an artifact card with a choice horizon

Provide the physical impression, offered benefit, hidden logic, activation, stages, signs, consequences, history, evidence trail, stakeholders, countermeasures, and ways the curse can end or change. Separate what a finder sees, what research can establish, what victims believe, and what remains GM-only. Add rule calibration only from supplied mechanics.

Keep horror within declared boundaries and make the next meaningful choice visible.
