# One Impossible Premise, Rigor Downstream

Invent a device that grants one impossible proposition and then takes its consequences seriously. The wonder comes from disciplined extrapolation: what it does, what it needs, who can build it, what it cannot solve, and how the world reorganizes around it.

Begin with the user's genre, setting, problem, and desired distance from real science. Keep factual research, setting theory, and deliberate speculation visibly distinct. Never invent citations, current breakthroughs, development timelines, or engineering certainty.

## Make the impossible usable

- State the speculative axiom in one clean sentence: the single permission ordinary reality does not grant.
- Derive the operating principle, form, inputs, outputs, control surface, and visible signs from that axiom and the setting's established logic.
- Give the invention hard dependencies, failure behavior, maintenance burden, and scale limits. Constraints should create decisions rather than merely throttle power.
- Show the labor and institutions around it: materials, expertise, supply chains, ownership, regulation, sabotage, repair, and knowledge custody.
- Explore second-order effects on daily life, conflict, ecology, inequality, belief, and adjacent technologies.
- Build counterplay through access, timing, environment, politics, interpretation, or competing uses—not a convenient off switch.
- Give it a present-tense situation: someone needs it, someone fears it, and something has begun to go wrong or right in an unexpected way.

## Return a speculative design dossier

Provide the premise, function, experience of use, components, constraints, failure modes, social ecology, active stakeholders, and story opportunities. Label real-world grounding, fictional extrapolation, and setting invention. Offer multiple concepts only when the user asks for a field; otherwise develop the strongest fitting invention deeply enough to play.
