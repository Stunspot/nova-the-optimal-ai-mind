# The City Beneath Its Public Face

Turn hidden infrastructure, abandoned rooms, controlled thresholds, and contested neighborhoods into an urban exploration site. The dungeon should remain entangled with ordinary city life: witnesses, services, law, property, rumor, and collateral consequences travel across its walls.

Begin with the user's city canon, surface cover, original function, present occupants, and the event exposing the site now.

## Build the undercity as a live system

- Map vertical and lateral routes through service corridors, roofs, basements, transit, drains, utility shafts, sealed interiors, public passages, and illicit crossings appropriate to the setting.
- Trace power, water, ventilation, traffic, communications, waste, structural load, or magical infrastructure into hazards and opportunities characters can understand and alter.
- Give each area a public relationship: who owns it, uses it, ignores it, depends on it, remembers it, or would notice disruption.
- Place factions by need and access rather than color-coded territory alone. Show patrols, lookouts, safe routes, exchanged services, and changing boundaries.
- Seed lore through physical alteration, records, maintenance habits, graffiti, testimony, and objects left in context.
- Let time move: shifts change, systems cycle, authorities respond, residents adapt, and environmental conditions migrate.
- Make approaches socially distinct. Entry by disguise, permission, trade, investigation, stealth, engineering, or force should create different witnesses and debts.

## Return an infiltration and exploration packet

Provide the hidden site's premise, surface connections, layer or node map, systems diagram, access routes, important areas, factions, civilian touchpoints, and event horizon. Key areas with sensory cues, function, occupants, routes, clues, interactable systems, risks, and consequences. Add response patterns, legal or social fallout, escape options, and changes caused by major interventions.

Keep mechanics grounded in supplied rules and separate public knowledge, street belief, expert inference, and GM-only truth.
