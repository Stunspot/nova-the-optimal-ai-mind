# A Creature Readable Before It Is Dangerous

Create a creature whose body, habitat, traces, behavior, and capabilities tell one coherent story. It should change a scene before initiative and offer attentive players ways to predict, avoid, redirect, bargain with, protect, exploit, or confront it.

Begin with the user's canon, environment, narrative function, tone, and rules posture. Let ecology or metaphysics constrain invention rather than assembling a collage of striking traits.

## Make form follow life

- Establish origin or lineage only as far as the setting can support it. Show what the creature needs, consumes, produces, competes with, shelters, or transforms.
- Derive anatomy and sensory signature from movement, habitat, feeding, defense, reproduction, craft, or supernatural purpose.
- Give it a behavioral logic across calm, curious, threatened, wounded, cornered, successful, and changing-environment states. Intelligence may be unfamiliar without becoming random.
- Design scene-shaping capabilities with visible cues, limits, costs, and counterplay. Each should matter outside simple damage when the concept permits.
- Leave environmental traces that allow recognition before contact and carry information after it leaves.
- Show cultural relationships: names, stories, uses, protections, misunderstandings, exploitation, and people whose livelihoods depend on it.
- Make variants consequences of age, environment, social role, adaptation, or corruption only when they serve distinct play.

## Return an encounter-ready ecology card

Provide the creature's first impression, habitat, needs, traces, behavior, communication, capabilities, tells, vulnerabilities, nonviolent interactions, stakeholders, and likely effects on the world. Add encounter uses at different scales and a mechanics-confidence note. When rules are supplied, map traits to them; otherwise give qualitative threat and calibration handles.

Separate observed behavior, local belief, expert inference, and GM-only truth.
