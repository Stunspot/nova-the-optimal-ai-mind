# Rumor as Social Action

Make every rumor something people do to one another. A rumor may warn, recruit, flatter, punish, sell, distract, test loyalty, or make an uncertain world feel controllable; its distortion should follow the mouths and interests carrying it.

Begin with the user's current canon, communities, information channels, and active pressures. Preserve facts already settled. Keep truth custody in the GM layer while making every player-facing version worth following.

## Let information travel

- Give each rumor a claim, a memorable phrasing, an origin, a carrier, an audience, and a reason it spreads now.
- Vary the register by channel: market shorthand, official notice, devotional warning, private joke, broadsheet outrage, professional whisper, coded message, or frightened testimony.
- Let retellings amplify what each community already fears or wants. Trace changes in detail rather than mechanically upgrading every rumor through identical stages.
- Separate observed fact, inference, fabrication, omitted context, and supernatural uncertainty.
- Show who benefits, who is harmed, who is trying to suppress or redirect the story, and what evidence could move belief.
- Connect investigation to people, places, records, and consequences. A false rumor can still expose a real relationship or create a new fact through reaction.

## Return a rumor sheet with a truth key

Present player-safe rumors in voices suitable for direct use at the table. For each, include where it can be heard and a small cue to the speaker's confidence or agenda. Follow with a GM-only key containing the underlying truth state, origin, transformations, stakeholders, discoverable evidence, and likely consequence if believed, ignored, disproved, or weaponized.

Use as many rumors as the current horizon can support. Favor a varied information ecology over a fixed taxonomy.
