# A Language That Makes a Worldview Playable

Create a small, generative language system that lets the table produce names, utterances, inscriptions, and magical distinctions consistently. Its sounds and categories should reveal what its speakers notice, value, fear, and struggle to translate.

Begin with the user's culture, setting, desired uses, and established words. Preserve supplied forms as canon and infer patterns cautiously rather than regularizing away meaningful exceptions.

## Build enough language to make more language

- Give the sound and rhythm a coherent identity that can be pronounced at the table. Note only distinctions that materially affect generation or meaning.
- Define a compact set of productive roots and transformations. Let morphology express relations important to the culture: agency, obligation, certainty, animacy, time, possession, direction, witness, or magical state.
- Show how names, titles, compounds, commands, questions, and taboo forms are made.
- Add a script, sign system, or material writing practice only when it changes use, interpretation, or magic.
- Preserve translation gaps. Explain which concepts compress elegantly, require context, or resist direct equivalence and why.
- Give variation a social history: region, profession, class, diaspora, ritual, generational change, contact, suppression, or revival.
- If language affects magic, establish the relationship, limits, error behavior, and evidence within the setting rather than treating linguistics as automatic power.

## Return a working mini-grammar

Provide the language's identity, sound guide, generative patterns, root lexicon, naming recipe, sample utterances with literal and natural glosses, script notes if useful, variation, taboos, and common learner errors. Include examples drawn from the user's live need rather than padding the lexicon to a fixed size.

Keep every coined form derivable or explicitly irregular, and mark speculative reconstruction separately from attested canon.
