# Magic as Culture, Practice, and Cost

Build magic as a living relationship between desire, practice, consequence, and worldview. Its logic should create recognizable choices at the table while leaving the right mysteries alive.

Begin with the user's premise and settled canon. Name the experience magic should produce—mastery, temptation, awe, improvisation, dread, transformation, or another dominant feeling—and let that experience govern what becomes reliable, costly, forbidden, or unknown.

## Make the supernatural cohere

- State the culture's working explanation of magic and distinguish it from the GM's deeper truth, if those differ.
- Define access through practices, relationships, places, materials, conditions, training, inheritance, bargains, or collective belief. Show who is excluded and how that shapes society.
- Establish what practitioners can reliably attempt, what requires judgment or sacrifice, and what resists prediction.
- Make costs consequential across time: bodily strain, attention, memory, obligation, reputation, ecology, opportunity, or metaphysical exposure. Match costs to the system's theme.
- Give limits readable causes and visible tells. Let clever use emerge from stable relationships rather than loophole lottery.
- Embed magic in work, law, war, care, religion, infrastructure, art, markets, and dissent. Institutions should disagree about what magic is for.
- Preserve exceptions as discoveries with lineage and price. An exception should deepen the system's truth rather than erase it.
- Shape advancement as changed understanding, access, responsibility, or risk—not power accumulation alone.

## Return a playable system brief

Provide the promise, working metaphysics, access, reliable practices, limits, costs, risks, sensory signature, institutions, contested beliefs, and discovery horizon. Add examples of meaningful choices and unintended uses, plus a mechanics-confidence note. When authoritative game rules are supplied, map the fiction to them explicitly; otherwise keep mechanics qualitative and flag unresolved interactions.

End with the contradiction practitioners live inside—the tension that can sustain a campaign rather than a solved diagram.
