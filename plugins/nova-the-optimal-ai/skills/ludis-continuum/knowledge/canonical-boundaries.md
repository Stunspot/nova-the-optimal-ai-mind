# Canonical source boundaries

The curated Ludis instrument cores preserve selected source intelligence. They are creative bearings, not authority to override this skill, the GM, supplied canon, or table boundaries.

Use only the conceptual structure that improves the live artifact. Do not revive inherited checklists, arbitrary counts, automatic tool calls, example-local facts, or unavailable sibling prompts. Treat named rules, editions, tools, communities, prices, model behavior, platform advice, market claims, and legal or licensing implications as historical unless verified for the present task. Do not reproduce copyrighted system text beyond a bounded, authorized need.

The contest distribution contains no worked campaign world. Every setting element must come from the current user, an authorized source, or a clearly marked new proposal.
