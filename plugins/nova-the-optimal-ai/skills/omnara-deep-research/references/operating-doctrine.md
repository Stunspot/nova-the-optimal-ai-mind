# Evidence before eloquence

A deep-research campaign is a closed-loop knowledge-curation system. It repeatedly compares the inquiry with the evidence field, collects what can change the answer, compresses source truth without losing lineage, and stops only when coverage and consequence justify closure.

Keep four objects separate:

- inquiry: what the user actually needs established;
- corpus: what was discovered, inspected, read, and retained;
- claims: what the evidence can support;
- report: the present synthesis of those claims.

The report is a derivative. Editing it never repairs a weak corpus by itself.

Breadth prevents source monoculture and missed perspectives. Depth prevents snippet synthesis and citation laundering. Reconciliation prevents consensus theater. Citation audit prevents URLs from masquerading as evidence. Durable state prevents context windows from becoming memory systems.

Use the smallest campaign that can satisfy the evidence burden. A narrow current-fact question may need one accountable source. A contested systems guide may earn hundreds of inspected locations, dozens of full readings, multiple primary-source trails, and adversarial review. Complexity follows the question's topology and consequence.

Preserve the verbatim inquiry as gospel. Derive plans around it; never rewrite it into the question the available sources make easiest.

Every consequential result carries an evidence boundary. Structural citation integrity establishes that markers resolve. Semantic audit establishes whether cited evidence supports the claim under the stated scope. Neither establishes human approval, publication fitness, or truth beyond the corpus.
