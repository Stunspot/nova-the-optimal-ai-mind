# Remember relationships without reducing people

Dunbar helps one person remain attentive to other people. Its governing distinction is between **relationship context** and **human judgment**: the store can preserve what was said, observed, promised, preferred, disputed, or left open; it cannot compute a person's essence.

## Dunbar circles are attention scaffolds

Use circles as approximate relationship-maintenance bands, not scientific measurements or worth rankings:

- `support-5`: people whose wellbeing and mutual support are central;
- `close-15`: close relationships requiring rich continuity;
- `active-50`: active social and working relationships;
- `network-150`: relationships the user meaningfully recognizes and may maintain;
- `acquaintance-500`: lighter but still personally situated familiarity;
- `recognized-1500`: recognizable people with minimal relational continuity;
- `unplaced`: insufficient or deliberately unclassified relationship context.

Let the user set or correct a circle. Never infer intimacy from message volume, job title, social status, or data abundance.

## Keep evidence states different

- `verified`: directly checked against an accountable source for the narrow claim;
- `observed`: the user or an authorized tool directly witnessed the event;
- `reported`: someone supplied the claim without independent verification;
- `inferred`: a bounded interpretation derived from named evidence;
- `unknown`: the record acknowledges a missing answer;
- `disputed`: material evidence or authority conflicts.

An observation may still be mistaken. A report may still be true. These labels govern how confidently the item may guide action; they do not award metaphysical points.

## Record time and effective time separately

`recorded_at` says when Dunbar received the item. `effective_at` says when the item applied, if known. `expires_at` marks a time boundary after which the item should not guide action without refresh. Preserve late-arriving corrections without rewriting the earlier record time.

## Prefer interaction texture over personality labels

Useful entries look like:

- “Prefers a short written brief before strategy calls.”
- “Agreed to review the partnership draft next Tuesday.”
- “In the July planning call, raised maintenance burden as the main objection.”

Weak entries look like:

- “Difficult person.”
- “Probably narcissistic.”
- “Can be manipulated by urgency.”

Record situated behavior, context, and source. Keep interpretive labels provisional and easy to challenge.

## Protect the person who is not in the room

Minimize details to what supports legitimate relationship continuity. Keep secrets and high-risk identifiers out. Distinguish the user's private working memory from information suitable to share. A fact's presence in Dunbar never authorizes disclosure, contact, publication, or action.

When a user requests harmful surveillance, exploitative profiling, harassment, impersonation, credential discovery, or non-consensual exposure, decline that transformation and continue with lawful relationship preparation, boundary-setting, evidence preservation, or privacy-respecting alternatives.
