# Make context feel ready, not dumped

Progressive disclosure is a relevance discipline, not a formatting trick. Retrieve enough to avoid amnesia; reveal only what improves the present exchange.

## Silent readiness

Resolve the person and assemble a cue packet when a known name first matters. Keep it silent when the user is merely quoting text, the person detail would not change the answer, or the cue has already appeared in this conversational encounter.

Silence does not mean forgetting. The packet remains available for the next turn.

## Cue

Use a cue for a first relevant mention, a quick reminder, or a narrow question. Keep it to:

1. identity or disambiguation;
2. the one-line reason this person matters now;
3. zero to three current items selected for the live wording;
4. a light expansion affordance such as “I have the brief or full history if useful.”

Omit empty headings. When the record is an identity shell, say: “Recognized; no grounded details are recorded yet.”

## Brief

Use a brief for meeting preparation, “remind me about them,” relationship continuity, or a multi-part current decision. Prefer this order when populated:

- current relationship and Dunbar circle;
- active projects or shared context;
- preferences and communication texture;
- latest meaningful interaction;
- commitments, open loops, and time-sensitive follow-up;
- contradictions, disputed items, staleness, or missing evidence.

Cap the brief at the evidence that could affect the present decision. Preserve item IDs beside consequential claims when challenge or correction is plausible.

## Dossier

Use a dossier only when the user asks for the full record, history, provenance, correction audit, or relationship map. Include aliases, active and historical items, sources, relations, effective and record times, contradictions, supersession chains, and restricted material only when explicitly requested.

A dossier is comprehensive within the selected person and sensitivity boundary, not a license to expose unrelated third-party data.

## Relevance beats recency

Prefer material that changes the current action. Then prefer active commitments, open loops, pinned importance, and recent interactions. Use category diversity so three near-duplicate notes do not crowd out the relationship shape. Keep caveats adjacent to the item they qualify.

Do not force disclosure depth upward because the store contains more. More data is not more relevance; sometimes it is merely a larger haystack wearing confidence trousers.
