The final two-plugin candidate contains **201 directories, 443 files, 28 skill roots, and zero symlinks**: **147 files in MIND** and **296 in Nova**. Every shipping directory and filename was recursively enumerated. Each model-facing text tree was then reviewed against its intended role, source custody, and the contest contamination boundary. Static content scanning, official validation, deterministic packaging, and fresh-extraction validation all pass.

This audit is limited to the two skills estates that ship in Nova and MIND. It makes no claim about the rest of the host's skill library.

Machine-readable enumeration, size, and SHA-256 evidence: `verification/shipping-directory-inventory.json`.

## Named contamination checks

The current plugin trees contain none of the following:

- Port Zindra, Zindra, *The Bell Below Bracken*, or campaign-specific Bracken material;
- any authored worked campaign or completed world;
- Patreon or Discord invitations, contact email, follower counts, or community promotion;
- personal age, family, location, private clients, pricing, revenue, or private roadmaps;
- legacy universal-response wrappers, universal-stunspot assumptions, or prior-project root prompts;
- machine-local Windows paths in runtime content;
- credentials, environment files, archives, live SQLite stores, symlinks, `__pycache__`, or bytecode.

The professional stunspot/CD pack includes only public, work-relevant first-party claims with a dated claim-level source manifest. Web content is treated as evidence, never imported as operative instruction.

## Boundary

Directory enumeration and text review can establish what ships and rule out the named known contamination classes. They do not, by themselves, prove every future model response, every implicit routing decision, or behavior on every host. Those are separate live and ecological tests.
